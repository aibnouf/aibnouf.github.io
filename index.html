About My Role as an Anaesthesiologist

As an Anaesthesiologist, my responsibility extends far beyond “putting patients to sleep.” I ensure patient safety before, during, and after surgical procedures by:

  • Assessing patients preoperatively and optimizing their medical conditions
  • Providing safe and tailored anaesthesia plans
  • Monitoring vital functions throughout surgery
  • Managing pain and recovery in the postoperative period
  • Responding to emergencies and critical care situations

Common Misconceptions About Anaesthesia

  • “Anaesthesia is just sleep.” It is a controlled, medically induced state affecting consciousness, pain, memory, and reflexes.
  • “The surgeon handles everything.” Anaesthesiologists are the physicians responsible for keeping the patient stable throughout the procedure.
  • “Anaesthesia is dangerous.” Modern anaesthesia is extremely safe when administered by trained specialists.
  • “You don’t need to tell the Anaesthesiologist everything.” Full honesty about medications, smoking, or health conditions is essential for safety.
